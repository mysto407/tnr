Drugyal Dzong

Where Legends Meet the Mountains

Nestled against the backdrop of the majestic Himalayas, Drugyal Dzong is a historic fortress that tells tales of Bhutan's ancient past. Built in 1649 to commemorate a Bhutanese victory over Tibetan invaders, the Dzong, though in ruins, offers a glimpse into the kingdom's rich history.

As you explore the site, you'll be treated to panoramic views of snow-capped peaks and rolling green valleys. It's a fantastic spot to learn about Bhutanese history, soak in the Himalayan landscapes, and capture breathtaking photographs.

Tiger's Nest Monastery

Hike to Spiritual Heights

Perched on the edge of a steep cliff, the Tiger's Nest Monastery, or Paro Taktsang, is one of Bhutan's most iconic and sacred sites. It's not just a monastery; it's a testament to the incredible devotion of the Bhutanese people.

To reach this awe-inspiring temple, you'll embark on a moderate trek through pristine forests and scenic vistas. The reward? A mesmerizing view of the monastery hanging precipitously above the Paro Valley. Legend has it that Guru Rinpoche, the Second Buddha, flew to this spot on the back of a tigress, making it a site of profound spiritual significance.

Kyichu Lhakhang

Sanctity in Simplicity

Kyichu Lhakhang, one of the oldest temples in Bhutan, exudes a sense of serenity and spiritual grace. This sacred site, built in the 7th century, holds historical and spiritual significance for the Bhutanese people.

Surrounded by prayer wheels and lush gardens, Kyichu Lhakhang offers visitors a chance to experience the tranquility of Bhutanese Buddhism. The interior of the temple features intricate murals and sculptures, making it a captivating place for those interested in Bhutanese culture and spirituality.
